## CD19 Expression Overview

**CD19 (CD19 molecule)** is a hallmark B-cell lineage marker. The expression data from Open Targets confirms this strongly, with expression concentrated in B-cell-rich tissues and B-cell subtypes.

### B-Cell Subtypes Ranked by Expression

Among the specific B-cell populations in the dataset:

1. **Naive B cells** (CD38⁻) — ~5,712 TPM — highest among defined B-cell subtypes
2. **Plasma cells** — ~5,511 TPM
3. **Memory B cells** — ~4,834 TPM
4. **Class-switched memory B cells** — ~3,560 TPM

So naive B cells show the highest CD19 RNA expression, followed closely by plasma cells and memory B cells. All B-cell subtypes show dramatically elevated expression (z-scores of +4) compared to non-B-cell lineages.

### Protein-Level Expression

At the protein level (from Human Protein Atlas), the highest CD19 staining (level 3, "high") is found in:
- **Lymph node** — germinal center cells and non-germinal center cells
- **Tonsil** — germinal center and non-germinal center cells
- **Spleen** — white pulp and red pulp cells
- **Appendix** — non-germinal center cells (level 3) and germinal center cells (level 2)

### Notably Absent Expression

CD19 is essentially absent (TPM ≤ 5, z-score -1) in T cells (CD4⁺, CD8⁺, thymocytes), NK cells, macrophages, erythroblasts, megakaryocyte progenitors, endothelial cells, and most non-immune tissues like brain, heart, and muscle — consistent with its role as a strict B-cell lineage marker.

This expression profile explains why CD19 is a primary target for B-cell malignancy therapies (e.g., blinatumomab, CAR-T therapies like tisagenlecleucel), as it is highly and selectively expressed across essentially all B-cell developmental stages from early B cells through to plasma cells.
