
 1. Disease Standardization & Normalization

  Problem:
  Clinical trials use inconsistent disease names:
  - "Heart Attack" vs "Myocardial Infarction" vs "MI"
  - "Kidney Disease" vs "Renal Disease"
  - "COVID-19" vs "Covid19" vs "SARS-CoV-2 infection"

  Solution with MONDO:
  All map to the same MONDO ID:
  - "Heart Attack" → MONDO:0005068
  - "Myocardial Infarction" → MONDO:0005068
  - "MI" → MONDO:0005068

  Benefit: Users can find ALL clinical trials for a disease regardless of naming variations.

  ---
  2. Disease Hierarchy & Relationships

  Example:
  MONDO provides hierarchical structure:
  ├── Cancer (MONDO:0004992)
      ├── Lung Cancer (MONDO:0008903)
          ├── Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (MONDO:0005233)
          ├── Small Cell Lung Cancer (MONDO:0008433)

  Benefit:
  - Search for "Cancer" finds ALL cancer subtypes
  - Navigate from specific disease to broader categories
  - Understand disease relationships

  ---
  3. Cross-Database Integration

  MONDO maps to multiple other databases:
  MONDO:0005148 (Type 2 Diabetes) maps to:
  ├── DOID:9352 (Disease Ontology)
  ├── ICD10CM:E11 (Medical billing codes)
  ├── UMLS:C0011860 (Unified Medical Language System)
  ├── MESH:D003924 (PubMed MeSH terms)
  ├── Orphanet:ORPHA:... (Rare diseases)

  Benefit: Bridge between clinical trials and other biomedical databases in biobtree.

  ---
  4. Enhanced Search & Discovery

  Without MONDO:
  User searches: "heart disease"
  Results: Only trials explicitly mentioning "heart disease"
  Misses: "cardiac disease", "coronary disease", "myocardial disease"

  With MONDO:
  User searches: "heart disease"
  → Maps to MONDO:0005267
  → Finds ALL trials linked to this MONDO ID
  → Includes: "cardiac disease", "heart failure", "coronary disease"

  Benefit: More comprehensive search results.

  ---
  5. Integration with Other Biobtree Data

  MONDO enables connections across biobtree datasets:

  Clinical Trial (NCT12345)
      ↓ condition: "Alzheimer's Disease"
      ↓ maps to MONDO:0004975
      ↓
  MONDO:0004975 (Alzheimer's)
      ↓ linked to
      ├── ChEMBL molecules (drug targets)
      ├── Genes/Proteins (from Ensembl, UniProt)
      ├── Pathways (from Reactome)
      ├── Publications (PubMed)
      └── Other trials for same disease

  Benefit: Build comprehensive disease-centric knowledge graphs.
